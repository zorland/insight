Daniel Silverstein - Graduate Research

Worked as part of the 1000+ scientist experiment using the LHC at CERN.

Over the course of 6 years, I got to work on many projects including hardware, firmware: focusing on increasing the performance of the algorithms and software: statistical analyses in the search for dark matter.

Algorithms for detector calibration

Optimized the algorithm used to calibrate the pixel detector. The data from 80 million outputs must be sequentially fit to logistic curves

My efforts increased the speed by 10X and increased the roboustness of the fit

A/B testing using Monte-Carlo

From year-to-year, the number of particles colliding increases.

I led efforts to understand the effect -- using Monte-Carlo simulations to compare events with fewer or more particles colliding

The metric - which was the yield of our analysis - was not significantly affected.

Machine learning in the search for dark matter

ATLAS has collected trillions of events

We select an extremely small subset after data cleaning and a simple decision tree.

I used the random forest and neural network classifiers to categorize events in data as either regular matter events or dark matter events.

The classifiers were trained on Monte-Carlo simulations

Data segmentation in the search for dark matter

In order to make the search process more practical (ie faster), the dark-matter-theories are segmented behaviorally

Some theories result in more energy, others in more particles, and so on.
